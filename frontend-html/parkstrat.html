Average Walking Time

Description: Calculates the average walking time from a parking lot to all of your class buildings and ranks the lots by lowest average time.

Pros

- Simple and balanced across all buildings

- Great when your classes are spread evenly across campus

- Easy to understand

Cons

- Doesn’t prioritize especially long walks or specific class preferences

Majority Strategy

Description: Finds the closest parking lot to each individual building and ranks lots by how many times they appear as the “closest” across all your classes.

*best used when excluding lots from your calculation

Pros

- Great for schedules that jump between distant buildings

- Useful if you want minimal walking to any class

- Easy to understand

Cons

- May favor lots that are closest to just one building

- Doesn’t account for overall walking effort — just count of appearances

Weighted Prioritization

Description: Lets you assign a priority value (1 to 3) to each class — the algorithm will weigh walking time more heavily for higher-priority classes (e.g. early morning or back-to-back classes).

Pros

- Gives you more control over your own schedule

- Ideal for minimizing effort around important or time-sensitive classes

Cons

- Slightly more effort to set up

- Might ignore lower-priority classes completely if weighting is skewed
